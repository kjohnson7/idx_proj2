QUANTIFIED SELF

An Interactive Data Visualization Project



As more and more applications and services are collecting data about everything happening in the world—from the most mundane like how many times you listened to Bruno Mars new song, to the tragic, like the number of refugees fleeing war-torn states, to the overwhelming, like the amount of traffic being routed through specific servers or being attacked with denial of service attacks—it is more important than ever for designers to be able to think about data and how it can be presented, made interactive and manipulated to tell a story or to guide someone to help them take action - at varying levels of need and interest.

In this project we took the approach of the Quantified Self as seen in the projects Dear Data and the annual reports put out for several years by Nick Felton, to help get a handle on working with small amounts of self-collected information.

Our team designed a series of data visualizations that tell an interesting story about what is happening with us. This website features each team member along with visualizations of a few of our daily activities.
